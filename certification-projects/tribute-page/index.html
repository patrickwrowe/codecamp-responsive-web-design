Introduction

[PLACEHOLDER] Dr. Patrick Rowe is a scientist with expertise in computational materials science, bioinformatics, and data science. His academic work includes the development of machine learning potentials for materials such as carbon and graphene, enabling more accurate molecular simulations. In his role at AbCellera, Patrick contributed to advancing cancer therapies by applying data science and bioinformatics to the development of bispecific T-cell engagers, with a focus on enhancing their clinical safety and efficacy. His work bridges theoretical research and practical applications, particularly in materials science and cancer immunotherapy.

Experience

May 2021 - Present, Protein Engineer, AbCellera, Canada

  • Development and application of in-silico methods for prediction of antibody properties, including developability, immunogenicity.
  • Algorithm development and application for lead candidate selection drawing from a range of experimental assay-based and computational metrics of antibody performance.
  • Collaboration with experimental team members to provide interpretations of experimental data, antibody properties and characterisation to improve lead candidate design.

Jan 2020 – March 2021, Industrial Research Fellow, University of Cambridge and Happy Electron, UK

  • Development and application of machine learning methodologies, e.g. Gaussian process regression, to novel problems, from atomistic modelling through to macroscale property prediction.
  • Large-scale data analytics, machine learning data set construction and data visualisation. Presentation and scientific communication to wide range of audiences, from specialist to non-technical.

2016 – 2020, PhD Physics, University College London (UCL), UK

  • Developed multiple world-leading machine learning atomistic potentials using Gaussian Approximation Potential methodology, resultant models are freely available online and have been adopted in academia and industry.
  • Worked on projects applying neural networks to the study of complex aqueous systems, developed methodologies for automation of training, model optimisation/selection and scoring.
  • Contributed to fundamental theory for development of future machine learning models for atomistic systems.
  • Lead theoretical/experimental collaborations between UCL and the National Graphene Institute leading to high-impact publications.

2014 - 2015, Research Placement (Full Time), DSM Speciality Resins, Zwolle, NL

  • Developed alternative to traditional petrochemical based polymers, achieving over 80% bio-renewable content in the finished product.
