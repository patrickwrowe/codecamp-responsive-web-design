GAP-20 Carbon Potential

Introduction

The GAP-20 AI model was built with the aim of performing highly accurate atomistic simulations of carbon. At the time of its publication it was the first general-purpose model for performing atomistic simulations using AI. It continues to be actively used for fundamental research, and is a benchmark for accuracy against which new models are compared.

GAP-20 uses the Gaussian approximation potential (GAP) methodology. It describes the properties of the bulk crystalline and amorphous phases, crystal surfaces, and defect structures with an accuracy approaching that of direct quantum mechanical simulation, but at a significantly reduced computational cost.
